Why to choose right course

  1. Career Direction
    Guides you toward your dream job or profession.
  2. Time & Cost Efficiency
    Prevents delays and extra expenses from switching courses.
  3. Better Performance
    You're more likely to succeed in subjects you enjoy.
  4. Job Opportunities
    Right course builds skills that employers actually need.
  5. Personal Satisfaction
    Makes your study experience enjoyable and meaningful.

What happen when choosing the wrong course

  1. Lack of Interest
    You may feel bored, unmotivated, or stressed.
  2. Wasted Time & Money
    You might need to drop out or switch, losing time and resources.
  3. Poor Performance
    Studying something you don’t enjoy often leads to low grades.
  4. Career Confusion
    You may struggle to find a job you like or feel stuck in an unwanted field.
  5. Mental Frustration
    Can affect your confidence and cause regret or anxiety.

How to Choose the Right Course (Step-by-Step)

  1. Know Your Interests
    • What subjects or activities do you enjoy?
    • What are you passionate or curious about?
  2. Identify Your Strengths
    • Are you better at science, math, communication, creativity, or hands-on work?
    • Choose a course that matches your natural skills.
  3. Set Your Career Goals
    • Think about what kind of job or lifestyle you want.
    • Research which courses lead to that career.
  4. Research the Course Details
    • Check what subjects are covered, how long it takes, and what skills you'll learn.
    • Look at the course curriculum, not just the title.
  5. Check Job Market Demand
    • Are there jobs available in the field you're choosing?
    • What is the expected salary or growth in that profession?
  6. Compare Institutions
    • Research colleges/universities offering the course.
    • Consider location, reputation, facilities, and scholarships.
  7. Talk to People
    • Speak with teachers, counselors, or students in the course.
    • Ask about their experiences and challenges.
  8. Consider Your Budget and Support
    • Can you afford the course (tuition, books, living costs)?
    • Do you have financial or family support?
